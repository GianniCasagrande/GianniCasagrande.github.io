Axonify Content UI

Research • Concept Creation • Visual Design • Prototyping • CSS • Testing

About

The Axonify content management system is a core component of the platform. Users come here to create and manage all the microlearning and training content they want to serve their employees. Content can include different question types (multiple choice, multi-answer, matching, fill in the blank), SCORM modules, video training, surveys, behaviour observations, tasks, key learning points and training plans.

Problem

This was a re-vamp of the old CMS that I designed when we were a startup. The old iteration of content was showing its age as it was a very click intensive bulky UX. It was also not supporting industry training best practices around strategic planning & design, key learning points, change audits and peer reviews. I was tasked with re-designing how users dealt with content, with a heavy emphasis on quick creation and easy maintenance of content.

From Left: Content Creation Editor, "Hotspot" question UX

Constraints

Had to respect the current content hierarchy setup (Category > Subject > Topic). Was not allowed to deviate from this as this was how most of our customers were creating content and migration would be too difficult. I also still needed to support a “click to edit and click to save” paradigm. Autosave was to large of a change from a server development point of view, especially for a v1.

High-Level Goals

  • Streamline content creation, editing and publishing
  • Bake in industry standards: training plan, key learning points
  • Integrate review process, including "off platform" user reviews i.e. lawyers
  • Import & Export content from excel, pdf & word
  • Audit trail with comments and timestamps
  • Easy content retrieval and searching
  • Full meta on all content items. ID, SKU, Location and number of containing items
  • New layout (This is becoming the base layout for all pages going forward)

Research

  • Conducted client interviews: how were they creating content? Where they using industry standards? What pain points were they experiencing with the current UX? What was missing from the current setup?
  • Working with the Product Management team, we created user personas to design against.
  • Internal instructional design interviews with Axonify instructional design service teams
  • Discussed how key learning points (KLPs) link to content and content difficulty levels
  • Interviewed how they were currently collaborating with customers
  • Discussed What was missing from the current process from a strategy point of view
  • UX prototype testing with internal and external stakeholders
UI Snippets from left: Topic Search, Multi-select menu, Content Reviewing, Training Plan, Audit & Commenting

Solution

For the new content UI I opted for a 2 panel “live” view. When you select an item on the left, you could see the "detail" of that item on the right. Details can include simple meta items like name and ID all the way to complicated items like question previews and training videos - all inline. Detail panes had an activity and comment feed for accoutability.

Key Experiences

  • Sreamlined layout
  • One click editing
  • Easy creation
  • Bulk operations
  • Searching and filtering of Content
  • Commenting and Auditing
  • Content review with review assignment
  • Keyboard navigation
  • 19+ language support + language content creation
